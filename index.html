Proximity Based Human-Robot Seamlessly Collaborations: A Framework for Close Interactions

Liana Bertoni, Luca Muratore, Lorenzo Baccelliere, Nikos Tsagarakis

Short Video Full Video

Abstract

In this work, we propose a human-robot collaboration system, which permits efficient seamless human-robot co-existence and collaboration in vicinity of the robot. By enabling the robotic system to be aware of its close workspace, the robot is able to adapt accordingly to prevent potential undesired contacts with the human operator. To provide this awareness and the ability to detect and negotiate potential contact in a short range of distance, appropriate sensing technologies such as distributed proximity sensors on the robot body are considered for monitoring the workspace then the presence of the human arm in the vicinity of the robot while it performs its motions to execute collaborative tasks with the human. Such proximity sensing arrangement has been incorporated on a custom lightweight robotic arm and a control framework has been developed to generate appropriate motion reactions when the human and robotic arms approach each other in close vicinity, while an impedance modulation strategy is also proposed to generate online the robot impedance settings based on the required motion precision and expected interaction force during the task execution. The proposed approach permits to execute collaborative tasks, enabling the robot to transparently share its workspace with the human operator in an efficient manner. To validate the proposed system, collaborative industrial tasks composed of sub-tasks allocated to the human and the robot and performed within shared regions of the workspace have been carried out demonstrating the efficacy of the approach.
