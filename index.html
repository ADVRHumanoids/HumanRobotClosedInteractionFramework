REAct - A semantic driven framework for Robot task Execution Awareness

Damiano Gasperini, Luca Muratore, Nikos Tsagarakis

Italian Institute of Technology
Video Data

Abstract

The advancement of autonomous robots is still in need of a comprehensive framework for task execution awareness, enabling the generation of autonomous responses in unknown dynamic environments. Referred to as situation awareness, this cognitive domain endeavors to enhance the robot's awareness of its surroundings while it performs a specific task. In this work, we propose a novel bio-inspired framework for Robot task Execution Awareness (REAct), addressing the intricate challenges of perceiving, navigating and manipulating dynamic environments. The combined utilization of online planning, reasoning and monitoring along with the perceived environment's semantic is central to REAct's objective of achieving effective robot awareness. It is designed as an online process, which commences with the collection and analysis of sensors data, progresses through a real-time decision-making phase, and culminates in the execution and monitoring of each action. Being highly modular and robot-agnostic, the proposed framework aims for scalability across diverse robotic platforms and tasks. Additionally, its implementation can be adapted to support a variety of ontologies, reasoning and planning languages. In this work, a ROS-based implementation of REAct comes along with demonstrative tasks to showcase its capabilities on CENTAURO robot, offering insights into its potential efficacy in real-world scenarios.
